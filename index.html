Blue Before you.

Growing up, I always got excited when blue lights whizzed by.
‘Look at those heroes risking their life to fight crime.’
Like the movies, like the shows.
I was young, how could I know?

I prepared myself for discipline
And God willing
I would be just like them
The Big Black Boots, The Helmet, The Shield, The Mace
The Stab Vest

But wait,
I thought a shield is meant protect?
Not oppress
When someone is distressed
Is it really the best idea to send someone they fear?
A domestic solider in militarised gear.
What good is it to lock teenagers in a cage?
Fill them with rage then tell them to behave.
Keep them dumb, keep them ignorant
They’re all guilty until proven innocent.

When you dissect this twisted system,
And see the effect on their victims
It’s hard not to lose faith
When they do not defuse hate
But create it, embrace it, entice it,
Then price it
and send you the bill.
Pay those taxes and submit your will.

When the masses are calling for change
Who else can they blame?
Politicians make the order
But they carry out the slaughter.

Children of the Patriarchy.


I keep your father at work and your mother in the kitchen
Watch me grind them down into nothing more than a man and a woman.
Listen,

Boys will be boys; Girls will be girls.
But boys will employ, and girls will be cleaners
I train my sons to be killers and my daughters to be strippers.
I am the reason boys touch everything they see.
I am the reason girls can’t walk down moonlit streets.
And though I am at the heart of the problem,
it means nothing to me.

They call me The Patriarchy.

I have lasted throughout history,
And infected every part of society
Because I am cruel.
I said I am cruel.
Whether it is at home, at work, or in the school
you are all under my rule.
I make girls feel fat and make boys feel skinny
I am the only reason you can buy sex for money
I like my men angry, my women slutty,
And a nice pair of tits on page 3.
Every black eye covered in makeup is because of me
Can’t you see?
I am vile
I will defile your children:

Keep those fists clenched, boy. Hold your shoulders high.
Never back down and don’t you ever cry.
Only cowards think of suicide.
You will learn how to punch before you can crawl.
You will learn to chase girls like they are a football.

Shut your mouth, girl. You should learn to be quiet.
Look at those girls on Instagram, maybe you should diet?
That model looks fit in that dress, maybe you should buy it?
You will learn how to clean before you can read
You can try for a job, but you will never succeed.

I Am Everywhere
Like A Virus

Decay of an Oasis


A flood of monotony and
whitewashed walls try to smother her
But her fire burns bright on this Turbo Island
Like a beacon
And the drums
The drums keep pounding

Shields, batons, helmets for hire
Watch like hawks
As teddy-bear rioters paint her skin
And they dance
As the drums
The drums keep pounding

Things are changing in the mild mild west
But her walls remain a mural of identity
And her song will always shake the streets
With relentless optimism
The drums
The drums keep pounding

They pound by day; they pound by night.
They will not die without a fight
